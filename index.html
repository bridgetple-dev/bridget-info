WHY BRIDGET

Bridges Yet to Be Built
Between Hundreds of Languages

One in five children worldwide — 412 million — live on less than $3 a day. 417 million grow up deprived of two or more basic needs: education, health, nutrition, and sanitation. Education aid is set to be cut by 24%, putting 6 million more children at risk of being pushed out of school.

UNICEF warns that "digital technology is a gateway — without it, disparities only deepen." Yet the world's best educational content — textbooks, animations, video lectures, interactive tools — exists almost entirely in a handful of languages.

BRIDGET tackles this through AI translation and community participation. We're building a pipeline where anyone can help translate educational content into every language — books, videos, subtitles, apps — and every contribution is verified and improved through feedback.

* UNICEF, State of the World's Children 2025

Where We Are Right Now

BRIDGET is in active development. Here's what's built, what's next, and where we need help.

check_circle

Translation Pipeline — Built

7-agent AI pipeline with MQM quality verification. Tested across 8 languages on educational content — average quality score 95.2, zero critical errors.

check_circle

E-book Reader — Demo Ready

Custom-built reader for translated books. Spatial memory, multi-language support, and accessibility features.

construction

Community Translation Process — Designing

Building a pipeline where anyone can contribute to translation — review, correct, improve. The AI does the first pass; the community makes it perfect. This is the core of what we're designing right now.

schedule

More Formats — Planned

Video subtitles, animations, educational apps, interactive content. The pipeline is format-agnostic by design — expanding to new formats is a matter of integration, not rebuilding.

schedule

Accessible TTS — Planned

Text-to-speech for translated content, so visually impaired students can listen to any lesson in their language.
